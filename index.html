From circuits to robots…

Over the span of two weeks, you will go from only knowing how to build basic resistor circuits from ECE110 to working with transistors, op-amps, and microcontrollers to build your own line-tracing robot. Led by Professor Hamid Shokrollah-Timorabadi and upper-year ECE students, you'll gain hands-on experience with many of the topics covered in second year, giving you a head start before the fall semester.

You will learn about:

No textbooks. You learn it by building it.

Electronics & Circuit Design

Build and debug real circuits using oscilloscopes, multimeters, function generators, and soldering equipment.

Transistors, Diodes, Capacitors & Inductors

Learn how fundamental electronic components work, giving you a head start in 2nd-year courses like ECE231 and ECE212.

Embedded Systems

Program an Arduino-compatible microcontroller to read sensors, generate signals, control motors, and talk to external hardware.

CAD & 3D Printing

Learn the basics of Fusion 360 and 3D printing to design, manufacture, and assemble your own robot.

Robotics Competition

Bring your robot to life and race it head-to-head against the rest.
